The field Q of rational numbers forms a countable, dense subset of R, characterized by closure under addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division excluding division by zero. Every rational number r in Q satisfies a linear polynomial qx - p = 0 where p, q are integers and q is nonzero, making rationals the simplest algebraic numbers.
