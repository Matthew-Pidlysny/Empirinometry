\section{Ratio Prediction Framework and Validation}

\subsection{Prediction Formula Derivation}

The Pidlysnian Coefficient in ratio form provides a predictive tool for estimating geometric minimums:

\begin{theorem}[Ratio Prediction Formula]
For a geometric problem with characteristic dimension $d$ and constraint complexity $c$, the predicted minimum $m$ is:
\[m = \left\lceil \pidcoeff \cdot (d + c) \right\rceil = \left\lceil 0.6 \cdot (d + c) \right\rceil\]
\end{theorem}

\[\minplace \geq \lceil 0.6(d+c) \rceil\]

\begin{proof}

\begin{itemize}

    \item 4 underestimations (all by small amounts)
