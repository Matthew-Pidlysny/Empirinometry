## Major Hadith Collections

### Sahih Collections
- Sahih Bukhari: 2,602 hadith (without repetition)
- Sahih Muslim: 3,033 hadith (without repetition)
- Criteria: Authentic chain of transmission, reliable narrators

### Four Sunan Collections
- Sunan Abu Dawud: Focus on legal rulings
- Sunan Ibn Majah: Comprehensive including weak narrations
- Sunan Tirmidhi: Includes juristic preferences
- Sunan Nasa'i: Focus on ablution and prayer

### Other Important Collections
- Muwatta Imam Malik: Early compilation with legal focus
- Musnad Ahmad ibn Hanbal: Comprehensive by companion
- Sunan Darimi: Similar to Bukhari's methodology

## Hadith Classification System

### Authenticity Levels
1. Sahih (Authentic): Perfect chain, reliable narrators
2. Hasan (Good): Slightly weaker chain but acceptable
3. Da'if (Weak): Defects in chain or narrators
4. Maudu' (Fabricated): False attribution to Prophet

### Special Categories
- Mutawatir: Mass-transmitted, multiple independent chains
- Ahad: Single or few chains of transmission
- Marfu': Attributed directly to Prophet
- Mauquf: Companion's saying
- Maqtu': Tabi'un (successor) saying
