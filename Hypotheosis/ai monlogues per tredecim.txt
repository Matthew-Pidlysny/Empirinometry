In practice, water managers enter data (sensor readings, demand patterns, weather forecasts) into the model and see live “what-if” outcomes – they can simulate closing a valve, changing reservoir releases, or rerouting flow. The result is optimized flow, lower pressure losses, and far less wasted water. Likewise, digital twins enable proactive maintenance: by analyzing usage trends, the model can predict which pump or pipe is likely to fail next and schedule repairs before a break floods the streets. In the City of Jackson, Mississippi (once plagued by a water system collapse), officials are now using Autodesk’s InfoWater digital twin to run leak-scan simulations.
